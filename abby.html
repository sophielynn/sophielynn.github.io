DESIGN BRIEF

—

HEADS UP: This is an exploratory topic of future interaction mode.

For designers of the new generation, learning how to get along with emerging technologies is a must. Conversational Technology is the one on the trend. People are starting to play with popular voice services including Google Assistant, Amazon Alexa as well as chatbot messaging services such as Facebook Messenger and Slack bots.
We will explore and understand how humans use conversation to communicate, be productive and experience the world. And We would like to apply Conversational Interfaces to common usage scenarios at Airbnb - expecting there would be some chemical reactions that can inspire us and other designers like you.

Conversational Interfaces

Natural Interaction
Voice is the most natural way human use to interact with each other.

Low Learning Curve
With flat information architecture, users no longer need to remember various paths to retrieve information.

Hands-free
Voice interface makes people hands-free. It is practical in some situation.

x

Airbnb

Heavy Information
User need to learn great amount of information from the website and the host. And the communication is through out the whole trip.

Rush Relationship Building
Users/hosts need to know each other in a short time and build good relationship.

Inconsistent Service
The service level of hosts varies, which leads to a expectation gap between hosts and guests.

How to build a chatbot that can serve as a supplement of Airbnb's current offerings is the challenge ahead of us.

VALUE PROPOSITION

—

We believe there are three major values for Airbnb to have Abby as its chat bot service.
1. It helps guests solve low-level issues effectively.
2. It's an illustration of hosts' personality and hospitality in another dimensions.
3. It's a method for Airbnb to control the quality of their service in a consistent yet customized manner.
